Where Good
Takes Root

A launch experience for the Cotton On Foundation
Singapore, late 2026

Scroll down to move through the night, one sense at a time. Tap a sense on the side to jump to its moment, and turn the sound on for the full effect.

Descend
The Threshold

The city softens
into green.

Guests step in off the street and the noise drops away. Before anyone says a word about the Foundation, they can already feel that the night is about something good.

The craftWe set the first impression with three things working at once. A cool green and blossom note in the air, lifted from the Foundation's own scents. A low wash of warm light. A quiet bed of water and leaves running under the room.
Pillar one, Learning

A room that feels
like learning.

This is where the education work lives for the night. Scholarships, schools and reading programmes across the region, gathered inside a pool of warm light. Everything you touch here is honest: recycled paper, woven cloth, raw timber.

The craftThe materials carry the message. Every surface in this zone is recycled or reclaimed, so guests feel the ethics of the work in their hands instead of reading it off a sign.
Pillar two, Wellbeing

A quieter place
to breathe.

For the mental health programmes, the room slows down. The light cools, the sound thins out, and a clean note of green tea and water gives everyone permission to stop for a moment. It is a small taste of the calm these programmes work to protect.

The craftNothing here is loud. The sound sits low and unhurried and the scent stays clean, so the calm feels designed rather than decorated.
Pillar three, Green Cities

The canopy.

At the centre of the room stands a living wall, the Green Cities project grown indoors. Light comes through it in shafts, the way late sun moves through real trees. This is the Foundation's environmental work turned into a place you can stand inside.

The craftReal plants, layered canopy light and the smell of soil after rain. This is the high point of the night, and it lands just before the celebration begins.
The Tasting Garden

Taste from the
same garden.

A signature Green Cities pour, botanical and herbal, made garden to glass and served zero waste by our licensed partners. There is a spirited version and an alcohol free one, side by side. It is the fifth sense, and usually the one people remember on the way out.

The craftThe flavour is built to match the room: green, clean and bright with citrus, with a nod to the Foundation's own mints, so taste and scent tell the same story.
The Reveal

And then the
light lifts.

Only now does the room brighten. The launch tee rises on a single plinth, the kinder, braver world design made with the Born This Way Foundation, with every cent of profit going back to the Cotton On Foundation. The clothes are the thank you the evening has earned, not the reason it started.

The craftLight, sound and scent all step up at once. The lift makes the range feel like a gift to guests rather than a sales pitch.

The Pledge

What you carry home.

Guests leave with a Foundation tote, the launch tee and a small Green Cities sapling. They also leave with a memory that sits at the level of feeling. Good, given somewhere to grow.
